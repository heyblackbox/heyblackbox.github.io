Building and maintaining design systems

Summary

  • I have created and supported design systems in two different companies where I worked as a design leader.
  • I have defined goals, acquired stakeholder and engineering buy-in, developed execution strategies, set up processes and teams related to design systems.
  • Designed the system together with its users: engineers and designers.
  • Developed and promoted usage policies across the organisation.

Many teams and products benefit from having a design system, but every team and product is different. Here I must quote a famous “Gall’s Law,” that applies to building design systems more than to anything else:

“A complex system that works is invariably found to have evolved from a simple system that worked. A complex system designed from scratch never works and cannot be patched up to make it work. You have to start over with a working simple system.”

Particularly important is the definition of a “system that works.” It’s tempting to copy a readymade design framework and expect that it’s going to work out of the box. Unfortunately, I have not seen it happening in case of design systems.

As with any set of guidelines, the design systems only make sense as long as people are actually applying them and they become an integral part of their workflow. Making people change their behaviors, not building libraries or patterns, is a real task of a designer of such systems. The design system is an internal product with a distributed ownership, and the designer has to create mechanisms of cooperation and collaboration.

Principles of designing design systems, based on my experience:

  • Before starting, take enough time. Мake sure everyone shares the goals and vision. Listen to people’s opinions, take enough time to reframe and rethink. Get everyone’s approval on a conceptual model.
  • Fix the status quo and evolve it gradually towards the vision, making sure every change is actually working.
  • Make acceptance a priority above artifacts, and over time the artifacts will appear.
  • First create a component in a library, then reuse it in a product.
