Fonoteka

A simple app that allows you to take
your favorite music albums with you.


My role in this project

Product design concept · Prototypes · UX · UI

Unbunding music

Fonoteka was made to become a part of the ecosystem of multiple applications, each app focused on a particular type of user behavior.

The goal of Fonoteka is to bring back the importance of music and album as a form of art.

User problems

I reflected on my personal ways of listening and interactions with music. I asked my friends, especially those whom I consider music experts. I talked to my colleagues and some random people. A number of keypoints was set:

  • During a daily commute there's not much time and attention for music discovery. Discovery mostly happens on web.
  • People who are seriously into music still prefer albums to automated streams of "recommended content".
  • Glitches and interruptions on mobile network are very annoying.
  • At any given point in time, normally there's a "personal top chart" of 3-4-5 albums, current favorites.

Inspiration

Car CD changers of the past. It was horrible to go around and load CDs into the trunk, but there was a good side of it: more attention to music. You listen to what you have in the trunk, and you get really close to it. Not like radio hits.


Hardware players with physical buttons. Toys. Actions that follow natural movements. Fluid interface is another way to get closer to the medium. Application or device can be as simple as a toy, but should work for real. Music control interface could be used while cycling in a heavy traffic, preferably without looking at the screen.

App look and feel

Concept: Limited slots + gestures

  • No streaming, instead “borrow” up to 5 albums per day to listen offline.
  • Limited number of slots to put albums — “CD changer” experience.
  • Search of select music from your collection on Zvooq.
  • Minimum buttons, maximum gestures for easy on-the-go operations.
  • Subscription price — $1 due to limited number of offline slots



Business requirements

  • Monetization model: free trial + paid subscription. Subscription price must be as low as possible. Users in Russia’s highly pirated market were not ready to pay for full-featured service.
  • Increase the social acceptance of paid music vs piracy.
  • Add value for both web and mobile parts of Zvooq music service.

Further improvements

After the first release, we improved a few things in the UX and monetization:

  • Discovery for new users. The app worked nicely for existing users who had a collection of music from web. For new users it offered only a search field to select new music. So we added a row of horizontally swipeable recommendations, to make it easier for new users to start.
  • One price tier with a recurring subscription. In the first release the app offered two one-time purchases: $1 for 3 slots and $2 for 7 slots. Quite soon it became clear that 3 slots just did not feel like something cool and worth paying for, and 7 slots felt much better, even a bit too wide. We switched to recurring subscription and managed to get better terms: $1 recurring payments and 5 slots.
