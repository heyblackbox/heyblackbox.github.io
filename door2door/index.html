Designing planning automation for a ridepooling service

Helping public transit companies manage uncertainty and minimize risk



Driver time is the most expensive resource in ridepooling operations. It's important to plan and efficiently use it, but also the time of the planner doing the plan is a very scarce resource, and it is usually not accounted for. Without planning passengers can not pre-book rides, and that ability is critical for passengers to consider switching from a private car to public transport.

My task

Understand the problem and create a concept that would enable planning without having to hire more people to support it, handle external changes as smooth as possible for the passengers, and unlock a potential market of larger scale operations.

Exploring the problem space

As a design lead for this project, I facilitated a series of workshops and interviews with customers (public transit operators) all over Germany. Structuring the research results helped find the right approach on the solution discovery phase.

We wanted to enable planning the behaviour of a complex system with multiple moving parts and external inputs. When a plan is a model of a future reality, with time, reality changes and diverges from the model, so the model has to be adjusted to match. In a series of internal sessions involving design, product, and leading developers, we figured out how the model should be organised to react sustainably.

Solution

Under my leadership we created a phased solution that bridged two valuable system capabilities (planning and pre-booking), addressed the pressing issues first, was able to smoothly handle external changes, and opened the potential for valuable optimisation in the future.
